Choosing Solo Selections.

The teacher and the student choose two appropriate pieces to be plated at the festival. See Suggested Class Levels to determine what level your student is.

Selections should be kept under 12 min. Long repeats should be omitted, except in short compositions such as at the Primary Level. Long Piano interludes should cut as well.

Piano accompaniment is required when applicable; CD accompaniment is not allowed. Students must list their accompianist on their entry form to avoid scheduling conflicts. Forms that do list an accompanist will not be accepted and may be subject to a $10 late fee.

All registered teachers are given an assignment to help judging, tuning and/or tabulation during the festival. Please select your judging preference when registering.

Choosing Duet and Ensemble Selections

Same as outlined above.

Gold Cup Competitions.

Students are awarded Gold Cups when they have earned 15 points, 30 points, 45 points, etc.

All solos selections must be memorized for Gold Cup Points. Duet and Ensemble entries may use music.

Gold Cup Points are recorded from the highest rating recieved if performing in more than one Duet, Quartet, or Ensemble. Points from different events cannot be combined such as solo and duet, duet and quartet, violin solo and viola solo, etc.

If a student has earned at least 10 points towards a Gold Cup, they may participate for one year after High School.

Studetns who have earned a Gold Cup will recieve a letter from the Gold Cup Chairman and are encouraged to participate in a recital where they will perform one selection from the festival and recieve their Gold Cup.
